We're creating the tools future software developers will use to build intelligent machines.
Modern AI is harder than it should be. Many of the current state-of-the-art systems are conceptually quite simple, yet require extensive knowledge of narrow frameworks, and often result in reinventing the wheel. Combining or integrating systems is extremely difficult due to a lack of standards, and the need to have a strong coding background and deep domain knowledge. As the future becomes more and more about complex architectures, we are going to see a rise in issues of collaboration and scaling if the current techniques don't change.
Nurture is an IDE that solves these problems, by introducing a modular, intuitive visual language for building AI pipelines. No coding knowledge is required, unless you want to build your own components, dramatically lowering the barrier of entry. It incorporates version control, not only for project architectures, but also for modules states, allowing for fast, easy experimentation and analysis of training. Components can be shared and combined like lego blocks, allowing you to create much more complex systems, greater than the sum of their parts.
No Coding Necessary
We are experimenting with various visual language prototypes, to create the most intuitive, easy way to describe AI architectures.
Open Modular System
We are building modules for everything from architectual components, to visualisation, training and I/O. Everyone can also build and share their own. Connect your system to anything.
Focus on Training
We understand that most time is spent on training, and have focused on providing the best tools and visualisers possible to run experiments, and debug your systems.
